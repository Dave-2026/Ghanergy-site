Project Overview

The Project is intended to provide a long-term infrastructure solution to two persistent challenges facing Greater Accra:

  • Increasing municipal solid waste volumes
  • The need for reliable, non-intermittent electricity generation
The proposed facility will receive separated municipal solid waste from Greater Accra transfer stations and other sources, recover recyclable materials where commercially viable, prepare a stable refuse-derived fuel stream, and generate electricity through proven moving-grate Waste-to-Energy technology.

The facility is being structured as a nationally strategic infrastructure project with strong alignment to Ghana’s waste management, energy security, public health, environmental and circular economy objectives.

This Project incorporates a number of structural, technical and commercial features that differentiate it from conventional waste-to-energy developments

Separated Waste Input

The Project is designed to process pre-separated municipal solid waste from transfer stations and other sources, reducing contamination risk, improving calorific consistency and enabling more stable plant performance compared to conventional mixed waste facilities.

Integrated MRF and RDF System

A front-end Material Recovery Facility and Refuse Derived Fuel preparation system is incorporated to recover recyclables, remove non-combustibles and condition the fuel stream prior to combustion, improving efficiency and reducing residual volumes.

Baseload Renewable Generation

Unlike intermittent renewable technologies, the Project provides reliable baseload power, contributing to grid stability while displacing fossil fuel-based thermal generation.

High Moisture Waste Capability

The plant is specifically designed for high-moisture waste conditions typical of tropical West Africa, supported by internationally proven moving-grate technology and waste blending systems.

Structured for Bankability

The Project is being developed under a PPP / BOOT framework with a clear ProjectCo / OperatorCo structure, designed to meet international lender requirements and support long-term institutional capital participation.

Ghanaian-Led with International Standards

The Project combines Ghanaian ownership and leadership with internationally accepted technology, engineering standards and environmental compliance frameworks.

Core Project Components

  • Enclosed waste reception and inspection facilities.
  • Primary waste bunker and blending systems.
  • Front-end Material Recovery Facility for recovery of recyclables and removal of unsuitable material.
  • Refuse Derived Fuel (RDF) preparation and controlled furnace feed systems.
  • Moving-grate Waste-to-Energy combustion system.
  • Steam turbine generator and grid export infrastructure.
  • Advanced flue gas treatment and continuous emissions monitoring.
  • Bottom ash handling, metals recovery and potential beneficial reuse pathways.

Expected Benefits for Ghana

Waste Management

Substantial reduction in landfill dependency and support for a more integrated waste management system in Greater Accra.

Energy Security

Reliable baseload electricity generation supporting Ghana’s power system and reducing dependence on fuel-based thermal generation.

Environmental Impact

Reduction in landfill methane, uncontrolled dumping impacts, groundwater risk and open waste management pressures.

Local Development

Creation of skilled jobs, Ghanaian technical capacity, local contractor participation and long-term operational capability.

Strategic Positioning

The Project is intended to operate as a central component of Greater Accra’s future integrated waste management system. It is designed to complement recycling, source separation, waste reduction and circular economy initiatives rather than compete with them.

By combining waste treatment with baseload renewable electricity generation, the Project provides a practical infrastructure platform for environmental improvement, energy resilience and private capital participation.

Development Status

The Project is at development stage and is being prepared for detailed Bankable Feasibility Study work, including technical validation, waste characterisation, environmental and social assessment, permitting pathway confirmation, commercial structuring and financial close preparation.

All technical, financial and regulatory assumptions remain subject to detailed feasibility review, government engagement, lender due diligence and final contractual approvals.
