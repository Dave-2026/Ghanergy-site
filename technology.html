Technology & Process Design

The proposed Ghanergy facility is based on internationally accepted moving-grate Waste-to-Energy technology, integrated with front-end material recovery and fuel preparation systems. The design is specifically adapted for high-moisture, heterogeneous waste conditions typical of tropical West Africa, while maintaining alignment with international environmental and operational standards.

Process Concept

The facility is designed as an integrated municipal solid waste processing and energy recovery plant. Separated municipal solid waste will be received, inspected, blended, processed through a Material Recovery Facility, conditioned into Refuse Derived Fuel, and combusted in a moving-grate furnace to generate steam and electricity.

Design Philosophy

The facility is designed to prioritise operational reliability, environmental compliance and feedstock flexibility. The process integrates waste reception, material recovery, fuel conditioning and energy recovery within a controlled and enclosed system, enabling stable performance under variable waste quality conditions.

Particular emphasis is placed on moisture management, calorific value stabilisation, emissions control and high plant availability, consistent with international Waste-to-Energy best practice.

1. Enclosed Waste Reception and Control

Enclosed waste reception, weighbridge control, inspection and bunker blending to manage moisture, odour and feedstock variability.

2. Front-End Material Recovery Facility (MRF)

Front-end MRF systems recover marketable recyclables and remove unsuitable materials before combustion.

3. Refuse Derived Fuel (RDF) Preparation

The combustible residual fraction is conditioned into RDF to stabilise furnace feed quality and improve operational consistency.

4. Moving-Grate Combustion

Proven moving-grate combustion technology is employed, providing robust performance across a wide range of calorific values, water content and feedstock variability.

5. Power Generation

Heat from combustion is recovered in a boiler to produce steam for a turbine generator exporting baseload power to the grid.

6. Emissions Control

Advanced flue gas treatment, continuous emissions monitoring and compliance systems are designed to meet Ghana EPA and international standards.

Design Basis

  • Separated municipal solid waste from Greater Accra transfer stations, commercial locations, and individual contractors.
  • Target throughput of approximately 3,000 tonnes per day.
  • Average received waste moisture content of approximately 45–55%.
  • Design net calorific value of approximately 8.5–9.5 MJ/kg.
  • Net electrical export capacity of approximately 60 MW.
  • Plant availability target of at least 90%.

Environmental Compliance

The reference design is intended to comply with Ghana Environmental Protection Agency requirements and to align with international lender expectations, including IFC Environmental, Health and Safety guidance and EU Best Available Techniques benchmarks for waste incineration.

Residues and By-Products

Bottom ash will be processed for metals recovery and may be treated for beneficial reuse subject to Ghana EPA approval and applicable engineering standards. Fly ash and air pollution control residues will be managed as controlled waste streams in accordance with regulatory requirements.

Proven Technology Platform

The selected technology platform is widely deployed internationally in facilities of comparable scale, including plants operating in Europe and tropical environments. The design approach is therefore based on established engineering solutions rather than unproven or experimental systems.
