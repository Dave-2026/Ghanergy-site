Investment Thesis

The project is intended to create a dual-revenue, non-merchant infrastructure asset. Base revenues are expected to come from electricity sales under a long-term power purchase arrangement and waste treatment fees linked to contracted municipal solid waste delivery.

The investment case is based on long-term contracted infrastructure cash flows, strategic alignment with Ghana’s waste and energy priorities, and a project structure designed to support international lender due diligence.

Why This is Investable

  • Contracted infrastructure model combining power purchase and municipal waste supply arrangements.
  • Dual revenue streams providing resilience and reduced reliance on a single income source.
  • Baseload renewable generation supporting grid stability and energy security.
  • Proven, internationally accepted technology adapted for high-moisture waste conditions.
  • Structured under a PPP / BOOT framework aligned with international project finance requirements.
  • Strong alignment with Ghana’s waste management, environmental and energy policy priorities.

Revenue Framework

Power Revenue

Electricity revenue is expected to be secured through a long-term power purchase agreement for reliable baseload electricity supplied to the Ghanaian power system.

Waste Treatment Fees

Waste treatment revenue is expected to be generated from separated municipal solid waste delivered to the facility under municipal supply arrangements.

Additional Value Streams

Recovery and sale of recyclables, metals and treated by-products, together with potential carbon-related instruments, may provide additional upside but are not relied upon in the base case.

Project Structure

The investment structure separates asset ownership and financing risk from development, construction interface and long-term operational execution.

  • ProjectCo is expected to act as the asset owner, concessionaire, financing vehicle and counterparty to key long-term contracts.
  • OperatorCo is expected to manage development execution, EPC interface, commissioning support and long-term operations and maintenance.
  • Government participation is proposed as a minority ProjectCo interest only, with no Government ownership of OperatorCo.
  • The structure is intended to support lender clarity, governance discipline and transparent risk allocation.

Bankable Feasibility Study

The next major development step is the Bankable Feasibility Study. This work is expected to validate technical assumptions, waste supply, site conditions, environmental and social requirements, permitting pathways, project economics, contractual structures and financing readiness.

Indicative BFS funding of approximately US$5.3–5.5 million is required, with repayment expected at successful financial close through the project financing structure.

Current Opportunity

The Project is currently progressing toward Bankable Feasibility Study and is seeking strategic investment participation to support this phase. The Bankable Feasibility Study is expected to provide the technical, environmental, commercial and financial validation required for full project financing and construction.

Early-stage participation is expected to provide exposure to the development phase of a nationally significant infrastructure project, with a structured pathway to participation in the long-term project capital structure at financial close.

Government Support Concept

The project is expected to require coordinated Government support through the PPP process, land arrangements, waste supply coordination, permitting, fiscal treatment and payment security mechanisms. Government support is expected to be primarily non-cash and subject to formal negotiation, approval and documentation.

Risk Allocation

The project is being developed around a risk allocation framework consistent with international PPP and project finance expectations. Construction risk is expected to sit primarily with the EPC contractor, operational risk with the operator under performance obligations, and political and change-in-law risks with the Government framework.
