Greater Accra Waste-to-Energy Project

The Greater Accra Waste-to-Energy Project is a large-scale, long-term PPP / BOOT infrastructure development that integrates municipal waste treatment with firm baseload power generation. The facility is designed to process separated MSW at scale, materially reduce landfill dependence, and deliver reliable electricity using proven, internationally deployed technology within a Ghanaian-majority ownership framework. The project is supported by international technical and investment partners with relevant experience in infrastructure delivery and energy projects.

Strategic rationale

One solution to dual structural constraints

Greater Accra faces a dual infrastructure challenge: rapidly increasing municipal waste volumes and a persistent national power deficit. The proposed ~60 MW Waste-to-Energy facility is designed to address both constraints through a single integrated solution, converting separated MSW into reliable baseload electricity while materially reducing landfill dependence.

Key Positioning

The project is presented as a lender-grade, investor-grade infrastructure platform aligned with international PPP standards and suitable for institutional debt funding and equity participation.

Primary drivers
  • Greater Accra currently generates approximately 5,000 tonnes per day of municipal solid waste. This will continue to increase with population growth.
  • Landfills at Kpone, Oblogo and Nsumia are at or beyond effective capacity.
  • Ghana continues to face a persistent 200–250 MW power deficit.
  • Reliable power remains linked to expensive imported fuel.
  • The project aligns directly with the 24-Hour Economy initiative and IWMS reform.
Technical overview

Process flow and treatment sequence

StepProcess ElementDescription
1Waste Reception & WeighbridgeControlled delivery, weighbridge recording, inspection lane, and enclosed tipping hall with negative pressure.
2Waste Bunker & HandlingCrane mixing, homogenisation, and feed control with ~7 days buffer storage.
3Front-End MRF (Materials Recovery Facility)Recovery of recyclables, removal of inerts, and pre-conditioning of feedstock.
4RDF Preparation (Refuse Derived Fuel)Shredding, screening, air classification, and final metal removal to stabilise calorific value.
5Moving-Grate CombustionRobust combustion of heterogeneous, high-moisture waste on proven moving-grate system.
6Boiler & Steam GenerationHeat recovery steam generator producing high-pressure steam for power generation.
7Steam Turbine & GeneratorElectricity generation (~60 MW net) with export to grid as firm baseload power.
8Flue Gas TreatmentMulti-stage treatment including Selective Non-Catalytic Reduction (SNCR) / Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR), lime dosing, activated carbon injection, and baghouse filtration in line with EU Best Available Techniques (BAT).
9Stack Emissions MonitoringContinuous emissions monitoring systems (CEMS) ensuring compliance with regulatory limits.
10Bottom Ash HandlingQuenching, metal recovery (ferrous/non-ferrous), and conditioning for reuse (e.g. road base).
11Air Pollution Control (APC) ResiduesCollection and safe disposal of fly ash and APC residues to compliant hazardous facilities.
12Water & Effluent ManagementClosed-loop water systems, leachate treatment, and controlled discharge in line with Ghana Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) standards.
Technical credibility

Proven technology and international benchmarks

The proposed facility is based on established moving-grate Waste-to-Energy technology, representing the global standard for large-scale municipal solid waste treatment. This technology is deployed across Europe and Asia in a substantial number of reference plants operating under stringent environmental, operational, and financing requirements.

Technology basis
  • Internationally accepted moving-grate combustion system designed for heterogeneous, high-moisture waste streams.
  • Proven performance across a wide range of calorific values, water content, and feedstock variability.
  • Integrated flue gas treatment systems meeting EU Best Available Techniques (BAT) emissions standards.
  • High availability design, typically exceeding 90% operational uptime in comparable plants in a range of environments.
Reference plant alignment
  • Technology aligned with modern European WtE facilities operating under EU Industrial Emissions Directive and related best practice standards.
  • Comparable scale, process method and configuration to established plants processing 2,000–4,000 t/day.
  • Integration of front-end MRF and RDF consistent with emerging best practice in waste preprocessing.
  • Design adapted for tropical conditions, including high moisture content and variable waste composition.
Operational robustness
  • Enclosed waste handling and negative pressure systems to control odour, water content and emissions.
  • Redundant critical systems to ensure continuous baseload operation.
  • Structured maintenance regimes aligned with OEM requirements.
  • Progressive localisation supported by international technical partners.
Risk mitigation

Structured approach to key project risks

The project has been structured with a clear risk allocation framework consistent with international PPP / BOOT infrastructure standards. Key technical, operational, regulatory, and supply chain risks are addressed through design, contractual structuring, and disciplined operational controls.

Feedstock risk
  • Multiple MMDA supply sources to ensure diversified waste inflow.
  • Minimum delivery commitments supported through municipal agreements.
  • Front-end MRF and RDF preparation to manage variability in waste composition.
  • On-site bunker storage (~7 days) to provide operational buffer.
Technical & operational risk
  • Proven moving-grate technology with extensive global operating history.
  • EPC and OEM alignment to ensure performance guarantees.
  • Redundant critical systems to maintain baseload availability.
  • Structured maintenance and lifecycle management planning.
Regulatory & environmental risk
  • Alignment with Ghana Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) permitting requirements and environmental standards.
  • Design based on EU BAT emissions benchmarks.
  • Enclosed waste handling and controlled emissions systems.
  • Early engagement with regulatory stakeholders and structured EIA process.
Commercial & structural risk
  • Long-term PPP / BOOT structure with defined contractual framework.
  • Separation of roles across ProjectCo and OperatorCo.
  • Government participation aligned with long-term project success.
  • Structured governance and oversight mechanisms.
Environmental, Social & Governance (ESG)

Sustainable infrastructure outcomes

The Greater Accra Waste-to-Energy project is designed to deliver measurable environmental and social outcomes aligned with international best practice, including IFC Environmental, Health and Safety (EHS) Guidelines and EU Best Available Techniques (BAT) standards, supporting institutional investment criteria.

Environmental Impact

The project incorporates a strong carbon abatement profile through both methane avoidance and fossil fuel displacement. This creates a credible basis for participation in international carbon markets under established methodologies, subject to certification and regulatory alignment.

  • Reduction of landfill volumes by approximately 90% of received waste, significantly extending landfill life.
  • Avoidance of methane emissions from uncontrolled waste decomposition.
  • Displacement of fossil fuel-based thermal power generation by reliable baseload green renewable output.
  • Controlled emissions using advanced flue gas treatment systems in line with EU BAT standards.
Social outcomes
  • Creation of over 500 direct and indirect jobs across operations and supply chain.
  • Formalisation and integration of waste collection and segregation value chains.
  • Improved public health outcomes through reduction in open dumping and burning.
  • Support for local capacity development through technology transfer, training and progressive localisation of operations.
Governance framework
  • Structured under a transparent Public-Private Partnership (PPP) / Build-Own-Operate-Transfer (BOOT) framework with defined risk allocation.
  • Alignment with Ghana EPA permitting requirements, Ghana Integrated Waste Management System and international compliance standards.
  • Robust operational oversight through ProjectCo and OperatorCo governance and management structures.
  • Commitment to anti-corruption, compliance, and reporting standards consistent with international investors.
