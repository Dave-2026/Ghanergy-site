Greater Accra Waste-to-Energy Project

Ghanergy Power Limited is developing a proposal for a ~60 MW Waste-to-Energy facility in Greater Accra, designed to convert separated municipal solid waste into reliable baseload renewable electricity.

Strategic Infrastructure for Ghana

The Project responds directly to two persistent national challenges: - chronic municipal solid waste pressure in Greater Accra - the need for reliable, non-intermittent electricity It is intended to support Ghana’s integrated waste management objectives, energy security, public health, local employment and environmental improvement.

Declaration

Ghanergy is committed to developing infrastructure that addresses Ghana’s social and structural challenges with discipline, transparency and long-term national benefit. The Project is designed to combine private capital, Ghanaian leadership and internationally proven technology to deliver a durable waste and energy solution.

Why the Project Matters

Waste

Reduces pressure on landfill and supports a more disciplined municipal waste system.

Power

Provides reliable baseload renewable output for Ghana’s electricity system.

Environment

Reduces landfill methane, uncontrolled dumping impacts and environmental risk.

Investment

Structured for long-term infrastructure finance, bankability and institutional participation.

Current Development Focus

The Project is progressing toward Bankable Feasibility Study, including technical validation, waste characterisation, environmental and social assessment, permitting, commercial structuring and financing preparation.
